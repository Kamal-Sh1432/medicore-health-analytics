2. Background & Business Context

MediCore Health Services operates multiple hospitals and clinics across India. Despite consistent growth in patient volumes, 
the organization is experiencing declining operational efficiency, rising patient dissatisfaction, 
and limited visibility into revenue performance.

Existing reporting processes are fragmented, manual, and reactive, resulting in:

Delayed executive decision-making

Inability to proactively identify revenue leakage

Suboptimal doctor utilization and scheduling

Absence of a unified and trusted data source

3. Business Problem Statement

The organization currently lacks a centralized, data-driven analytics platform that enables leadership and operations teams to:

Monitor end-to-end patient operations

Identify inefficiencies in scheduling and resource utilization

Measure and reduce revenue leakage

Support timely, data-backed executive decisions

4. Business Objectives

The key objectives of this initiative are to:

Reduce average patient wait time by 20%

Reduce appointment no-show rates by 5–7%

Improve doctor utilization by 8–10%

Establish a single source of truth for leadership reporting

Enable proactive operational interventions using predictive insights

5. Project Scope
5.1 In Scope

Patient registration and demographic data

Appointment scheduling and attendance data

Doctor availability and utilization metrics

Billing, payment, and revenue data

Historical data analysis (last 2–3 years)

Interactive dashboards and predictive insights

5.2 Out of Scope

EMR / clinical treatment data

Prescription and diagnostic analytics

External insurance processing systems

Third-party payer analytics

6. Delivery Approach — Agile (Scrum)

The project will be delivered using an Agile Scrum methodology to ensure incremental value delivery,
early insight realization, and continuous stakeholder feedback.

Key Agile principles applied:

Requirements refined iteratively through user stories

Dashboards and analytics delivered sprint-by-sprint

Business validation conducted at the end of each sprint

Predictive analytics introduced incrementally based on data readiness

10. Business Risks & Mitigation
Risk	Impact	Mitigation Strategy
Poor data quality	High	Data validation rules and reconciliation
Resistance to dashboard adoption	Medium	Training, sprint demos, stakeholder buy-in
KPI definition disputes	Medium	Early KPI sign-off and governance reviews
Change in business priorities	Medium	Agile backlog reprioritization
11. Success Criteria

Predictive model accuracy and business usability
